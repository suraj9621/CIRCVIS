About CIRCVIS

Context-Aware Waste
Classification System

We built CIRCVIS to solve a hard, real problem — waste that doesn't get sorted costs the planet billions of tonnes of recyclable material every year.

The Problem We Solve

Rapid urbanization has intensified municipal solid waste to 2.12 billion tons annually. Standard image-classification models fail in real deployment due to conditions that labs never replicate.

  • Only 32% of global waste is properly sorted today
  • 2 million tons/year sent to landfill unnecessarily
  • Facilities operate at 40–60% efficiency on average
  • High manual labour cost and workplace safety risk

Real-World Challenges

Mixed Waste

Multiple materials clumped together make single-label classification unreliable.

Partial Visibility

Overlapping and occluded items dramatically reduce single-model confidence.

Inconsistent Lighting

Variable illumination in landfills and MRF environments throws off colour-based features.

Material Degradation

Faded colours and worn textures from landfill conditions fool lab-trained models.

Our Solution

CIRCVIS uses an ensemble of three production deep-learning architectures trained exclusively on real-world datasets — not curated lab images.

Ensemble Deep Learning

  • ResNet50 — 88% accuracy, high precision server
  • MobileNetV2 — 84% accuracy, edge-ready at 13 MB
  • EfficientNetB0 — 87% accuracy, balanced cloud

Real-World Datasets

  • RealWaste — 657 MB of actual landfill imagery
  • TACO — 2M+ in-the-wild litter images
  • Combined — 2.5M+ images across 7 classes

Context-Aware Features

  • Data augmentation for degraded images
  • Attention mechanisms for occlusion handling
  • Scene context analysis

Deployment Scenarios

Robotic Waste Systems
